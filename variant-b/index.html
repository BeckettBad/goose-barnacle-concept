Brooklyn Roots

David's grandmother, Emma Sullivan, immigrated to Brooklyn from La Coruña, Spain in the late 1920's and the family has lived in the Cobble Hill‑Brooklyn Heights area ever since. In 1951, Sullivan opened The Long Island Bar, a diner on Atlantic Avenue.

Brooklyn is the place David's family has called home for nearly a hundred years and Goose Barnacle is just one part of the family's legacy on Atlantic Avenue.

La Coruña

The name of the shop, "Goose Barnacle," (in Spanish, percebe) comes from the Galician seafood delicacy harvested on the rocky shores of northwestern Spain. The silhouette of the famous Torre de Hércules lighthouse adorns the shop's sign.

From Brooklyn to La Coruña, Goose Barnacle's identity is drawn from a deep‑rooted connection to these two very different places.

David and Michelle

Step through the shop's antique wooden doors and you will find husband and wife team David Alperin and Michelle Lorenzutti welcoming you in with a smile. Here, you will discover Goose Barnacle's originals alongside a rotating, curated selection of worldwide brands.
